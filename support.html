Today

Today is a read-only overview of your day. It shows a hero card with your current or next upcoming task, followed by your task list for today. Yesterday's incomplete tasks appear below, and tomorrow's tasks appear after 18:00. You can't create tasks directly from this tab — it's designed for glancing at, not managing.

The hero card always shows your most relevant timed task.

• If a task is currently in progress, it shows that task. If there's less than 30 minutes left, a countdown appears.

• If a task starts within 15 minutes, the card switches to show the next task.

• If nothing is happening, the card shows a time-of-day message.

The "STARTS IN X MIN" label turns terracotta when you're 15 minutes or less away.

Tasks

Tap the + button at the bottom right of the Tasks tab or the Calendar tab. A sheet slides up from the bottom. Type your task name, then optionally add a date, time, area, repeat schedule, and mark it as urgent.

Tapping TIME in the task sheet opens a two-step picker:

1. Choose a start time preset — Morning, Noon, Afternoon, Evening, or Night.

2. Choose a duration — 30m, 1h, 1h 30m, or 2h. The end time is calculated automatically.

If you want an exact time, tap "+ Exact time" to use the manual stepper with − and + controls for hour and minute in 24-hour format.

Set a repeat rule when creating or editing a task: Daily, Weekdays, Weekly, or Custom (where you pick specific days of the week).

Day. generates upcoming instances automatically in a rolling 7-day window. Each instance is independent — completing or editing one doesn't affect the others unless you choose to apply changes to all instances.

Tap any task from the Tasks, Today, or Calendar tab to open the task detail page. All fields are editable inline — title, date, time, area, repeat, notes, and checklist. Changes save automatically.

Marking a task as urgent adds a terracotta "Urgent" pill to the task row and detail page. It's a visual signal — there's no separate urgent queue. Use it to flag things that need attention today.

Open the task detail page and tap the trash icon in the top right. If the task is part of a repeat series, you'll be asked whether to delete just this instance or all future instances.

When you set a time for a task, a NOTIFY field appears. You can choose to be notified at the time of the task, 15 minutes before, or 30 minutes before.

Day. will ask for notification permission the first time you set a reminder. The notification toggle in your profile will turn on automatically once permission is granted.

If you edit a task's time, the existing notification is cleared and you'll need to set a new one.

Field Notes

Field Notes is your space for long-form writing and voice capture. Think of it as a journal and a scratchpad combined — a place to think out loud, capture ideas, or record a voice memo. It's intentionally open-ended.

Tap the pen icon in the bottom right of the Field Notes tab. A new blank note opens immediately.

Press and hold the pen icon for about half a second. A recording overlay appears — a dark pill at the bottom with a live waveform and a timer.

• Tap Stop to finish recording. The audio is transcribed and a new note is created with the transcript as the body.

• Tap Cancel to discard the recording. No note is created.

Yes. Open any note and tap the mic icon in the top right. When you stop, the transcript is appended to your existing note body and the audio is saved to that note.

Transcription runs on-device using Apple's built-in speech recognition. After you stop recording, the audio is processed almost instantly. Because it uses native iOS speech recognition, accuracy depends on your device and ambient conditions. The transcript is fully editable.

Transcription uses your device's preferred language setting. Support and accuracy vary by language — this is determined by iOS, not Day.

Open the note. Audio recordings appear below the stats row. Each recording has a play/pause button, a waveform seekbar, and a duration display. Tap anywhere on the waveform to jump to that point, or drag to scrub through.

The stats row below the title shows: word count, estimated read time (based on ~200 words per minute), number of voice recordings attached, and the area assigned to the note.

Areas

Areas are personal organisational contexts — things like Work, Personal, Health, or whatever makes sense for you. You can assign one area to each task and each field note. They're colour-coded and appear as small dots throughout the app.

Go to Profile → Areas. Tap an existing area to edit its name or colour. Tap "New area" at the bottom to create one.

Day. will tell you how many tasks and notes are using that area before you confirm. If you proceed, the area is removed from all of them — they become unassigned. This cannot be undone.

Profile & Settings

In Profile, under Appearance, choose Light, Dark, or Auto. Auto follows your iPhone's system setting.

Day. offers two export options in Profile:

• Export Data — a lightweight JSON file with your tasks and field notes.

• Export with Audio — a zip file with your tasks, field notes, and all voice recordings. Use this for a full backup.

Both share via the iOS share sheet — save to Files, AirDrop to your Mac, or anywhere else.

In Profile, tap Import and select either a .json or .zip backup file previously exported from Day. Your tasks, notes, and audio recordings are restored. Existing entries are not deleted.

All data is stored locally on your device. There is no automatic cloud backup. Use Export with Audio regularly to back everything up — we recommend saving it to iCloud Drive or your Mac so it survives a reinstall or device change.

If you have a .json or .zip export saved, you can restore it via Profile → Import. If no backup exists, unfortunately the data cannot be recovered — local storage is cleared when an app is deleted. We recommend exporting regularly.
